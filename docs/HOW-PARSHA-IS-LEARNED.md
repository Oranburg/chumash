# How a parsha is learned

This is the chumash counterpart to havruta's `HOW-DAF-IS-LEARNED.md`. The app's features should follow the way the weekly portion is actually studied, not a generic reader.

## The week, not the day

The Torah is read on an annual cycle, one portion each Shabbat. The portion is announced as parashat hashavua, the portion of the week. Unlike the daf, it is a week's unit, but it is consumed across the week, which is what lets a weekly app still have something to open each day.

## The seven aliyot and the maftir

Each portion is divided for the Shabbat reading into seven sections, the aliyot, called up in order: Rishon, Sheni, Shlishi, Revi'i, Chamishi, Shishi, Shvi'i. A short maftir closes it. Traditionally a learner who reads ahead during the week takes one aliyah a day, Sunday through Friday, and the whole on Shabbat. That is the aliyah-a-day rhythm the app offers.

## Shnayim mikra v'echad targum

There is a long-standing practice of reading the parsha privately before Shabbat: each verse twice in the Hebrew (shnayim mikra) and once in the Aramaic of Targum Onkelos (v'echad targum). Many read Rashi in place of, or in addition to, the Targum. This practice is a progress structure waiting to be a feature: a per-verse tracker with three marks (first reading, second reading, Targum or Rashi) and a meter that fills as Shabbat approaches.

## The companions

Targum Onkelos sits beside the Hebrew as the oldest continuous translation. Rashi is the first commentary a reader meets and the natural expandable layer on a verse. The haftarah, a reading from the Prophets, follows the Torah reading and is chosen to echo it; pairing the portion with its haftarah and a short note on the connection is its own small feature.

## What the reader needs from the app

To meet the Hebrew: the text with vowels and cantillation, a translation revealed on a tap, and transliteration on first appearance for those reading along. To study it: the Targum and Rashi alongside. To keep the practice: the aliyah-a-day cadence and the shnayim mikra tracker. To round the week: the haftarah. These are the features, and each is a tracked issue.
